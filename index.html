For Women: By a Woman


~ SDG 5 aims at schieving gender equality and to empower women and girls to rise to the occasion and reach the rankings that men have achieved, mainly in leadership.

The percentage of males to female globally is nearly 50-50.

~ In many places globally, women are denied opportunities to work, study, and lead.

~ That means nearly half of the entire human population are denied human rights

~ This is mainly caused by gender biases, cultural norms and barriers in the institution. (CLI, 2025)

~ Women often struggle; not because that they are weak, but because they are judged differently from men.

~ And by denying women rights equal to men, people are denying half of the entire population to live a fulfilling life(globalgoals.org)

~ Because of this, we must make a change to this:

  • First, we can initiate negotiations with governments and organisations to enforce strict laws that allow women to have jobs

  • Second, by changing the cultural norms. By tackling the stereotypical roles of women and girls in the family and the community through education

  • Third, we can invest in initiatives that serve to protect and care for women and girls from gender-based violence

  • Fourth, we learn to accept the phases of women and girls during their lives instead of criticising them for it; in workspaces, schools and majorly in the society

  • And Fifth, we can close the wage gap between women and men so women can earn more and are able to afford for all of their needs

~ In conclusion, denying half of a population that can help change the world is denying the chance to welcome a better future for all males and females.

~ I hope for a world where everyone has the right to work, to learn, to live a fullfilled life.
